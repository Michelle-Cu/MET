About Michelle

I graduated from Taipei First Girls’ High School (TFG) and am currently studying in the Department of Computer Science and Information Engineering (CSIE) at National Taiwan University (NTU). I passed all four skills—listening, speaking, reading, and writing—of the GEPT (General English Proficiency Test) in sixth grade, obtained the TOEIC Gold Certificate with a score of 960 in ninth grade, and further demonstrated my strong language proficiency in senior high school by achieving an IELTS (International English Language Testing System) band score of 8.5. Across listening, speaking, reading, and writing, I am able to provide students with the most effective study plans and guidance.


My teaching principle is to foster a comfortable and encouraging learning environment, guiding students to develop interest in English, mathematics, and sciences, helping them practice with confidence and grow from their mistakes. I design each lesson according to students’ needs and goals, ensuring that learning is more efficient while also building a sense of achievement and self-confidence.


Whether it is preparing for examinations, improving English proficiency, or gaining confidence in daily conversations, I am committed to providing continuous support and guidance throughout the learning journey.
